News

04/2024: We secured a bronze medal at the 49th International Exhibition of Inventions held in Geneva, Switzerland. We showcased the invention ‘Building RoboAvatar’, in short, ‘BRobo’. With more and more robots used in the built environment, interoperable information between buildings and robots has never become more important. Those who develop the robots and those who design the buildings are two groups of people, and they use different software languages in their respective fields, which is a typical “Tower of Babel” dilemma. BRobo aims to tear down the “Babel Tower” between robots and buildings and provide a common language between robots and buildings to allow them to talk to each other. BRobo presents one of the first attempts to tackle this problem. In the future, BRobo can open many possibilities, including smart design, smart living, and smart inspection. The team includes Dr Junjie Chen, Prof Wilson Lu, Mr Yonglin Fu, Mr Xiang Ji, and me.

07/2023: I presented the research, “Determining near-optimal modularization solutions considering manufacturing operations in modular construction,” at the 2023 European Conference on Computing in Construction. In the implementation of modular construction, modularization is a crucial task that overwhelms designers. Different modular solutions influence manufacturing operations significantly, yet this has rarely been studied in the past. We proposed an optimization model that considered the operations of manufacturing and set-up changes.
